Concept

Legacy & the Future.
A living, breathing example of the Ralph Lauren world, where classic American heritage feels right at home alongside modern efficiency.

This regional satellite office is designed around Ralph Lauren's core identity: the layering of rich, heritage materials alongside a sleek, contemporary sensibility. Dark oak and brushed brass provide a traditional foundation, while black metal, stone, and glass add a modern counterpoint. Warm leathers and soft ivory textiles keep the space inviting rather than austere.

Ralph Lauren touches, tartans, stripes, equestrian art, are woven in subtly to ground the space in brand identity without overwhelming it. The result is an environment that serves equally as a productive workspace for staff and an impressive destination for visiting executives and clients.

Acoustic comfort and solar performance were treated as primary design constraints, not afterthoughts. Workstation orientation, material selection, and window treatment strategy were all calibrated in response to a rigorous sun study of the building's east west orientation.

Corporate Branding
02

Acoustic
Zoning Strategy

Armstrong Feltworks open cell panels target absorption at hoteling and collaboration zones. Woodworks grille ceilings soften open office reverberation. Metaphors coffers improve speech privacy in the board room and private offices.

Acoustics
03

Solar Performance
& Glare Control

A full sun study mapped morning east glare at hoteling stations and afternoon west heat gain in the lounge. Workstations were rotated perpendicular to the sun path, solar control film applied to east and west glazing, and roller shades specified throughout the open office.
