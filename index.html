About

"Interiors are a form of communication. Just as a good story has structure and flow, a successful space carries identity, guides behavior, and shapes memory."

Reagan Hack is an Interior Architecture + Design student at Queens University of Charlotte, bringing a rare cross-disciplinary background to the studio. Before design, Reagan built a career in public relations, marketing copywriting, and brand strategy — a foundation that now informs how she approaches spatial hierarchy, material legibility, and the clarity of the built environment.

A minor in Art History, Gallery & Museum Studies shapes Reagan's conviction that art is not an accessory but an integral architectural layer. As Curator and Panel Moderator at Queens University, she has organized two exhibitions — the Art & Design Faculty Showcase and Voices of Democracy — designing spatial layouts and facilitating live artist conversations for public audiences.

Fluent in AutoCAD, Revit, Enscape, and D5 Render, Reagan works across technical documentation and immersive visualization — with a growing practice in interactive design graphics and version-controlled project workflows.
